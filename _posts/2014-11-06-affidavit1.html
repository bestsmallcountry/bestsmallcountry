Sworn on 6th November 2014

Attachments:

  • Formal complaint sent to Mr. Francis Sheridan, Longford Co. Co. (28 Mar 2013)
  • Registered letter to Garda Commissioner Martin Callinan (06 Jan 2014)

  1. Unsubstantiated claims

    I hereby state that unsubstantiated claims were made about me to members of the board of Longford Sports and Leisure in Spring / Summer 2012.

    I have reason to believe some of these, to have been deliberately solicited. They were made to members of the Pool board by:

    • Mr. {% include m/david_sheridan.span content="David Sheridan" %}, the then acting pool manager.
    • Ms. Florrie Geelan (Newtownforbes).
    • Mr. Brian Gorman (Mohill, Co. Leitrim).
    • Mr. Atkins (may no longer be living in the area).

    Based on those claims alone, and with no opportunity to give my account of events to impartial arbitrators, a decision was taken by Mr. {% include m/dan_rooney.span content="Dan Rooney" %} on behalf of the board to suspend my membership for an indefinate period, terminating a valid pool membership.

    In telephone conversation with Cllr. Peggy Nolan in November 2012, I was assured that an apology for what had transpired up to that point was in the post. A week later, when nothing had been received, in another phone call to her, she repeated the same reassurance. Again nothing came.

  2. Suspension of membership

    Instead of an apology, I was notified of my suspension on 7 January 2013, on the morning of my first exam at the National College of Ireland. Instead of studying, I spent around two hours in a state of great distress, for the most part on the phone.

    Among those I rang were Cllr. Peggy Nolan and Ms. Mary Cooney (secretary to Longford County Manager, Mr. Tim Caffrey) twice. I requested a callback on both occasions. None was received.

    The following morning 8 January 2013, I again rang Cllr. Nolan only to be told that I'd been very unfair in my comments yesterday, she wished me well in my remaining exams and abruptly hung up the phone. I later concluded that her remarks meant that she felt threatened by my determination to establish whoever is responsible.

  3. Garda involvement

    I was impeded in trying to speak to pool users at The Mall, a public park where the pool is located, at 1.20pm on 25th March 2013. The Gardai were called at the behest of Mr. Dan Rooney as soon as I was seen speaking to swimmers I knew, who may have known something.

    Having failed to establish why I was moved on from The Mall, or the bounds of the restriction, eventually I met with the then Superintendent Denis Shiels at Longford Garda Station at 3pm on 24 July 2013. I was so devastated by his dismissiveness that eventually I complained to the then Garda Commissioner, Mr. Martin Callinan.

  4. Ombudsman involvement

    I made a complaint to the Ombudsman under file reference L30/13/1772. All opinions and emotions expressed therein, were those honestly felt by me at those times.

    The Ombudsman singularly failed in his duty, and did not investigate the complaint. Instead he placed the onus of proof on me, and in my opinion displayed no impartiality.

    I make this Affidavit conscientiously believing same to be true by virtue of the Statutary Declarations Act, 1938 and the Courts Act, 1964.

Sworn this 6th day of Nov 2014 by the said Mel McWeeney at Longford in the County of Longford before me a Commissioner for Oaths / Practicing Solicitor and I know the Deponent.
